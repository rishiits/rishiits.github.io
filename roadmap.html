Roadmap

Now, modern technology focuses on the properties of materials at the nanoscale, which has yielded a large amount of results. The impact of EUV(hyperlink) lasers, or lasers that can make transistors with nanomater-precision, allow billions of chips to be packed on a small, fingernail-sized chip, with 2-dimensional patterns only. Atomic-level transistors in 3-dimensions, with the same clock speeds (operations/sec) can have 10^9 (a billion) or more times greater computations per second, and that's ignoring the emergence of photonics. The current pinnacle of accuracy in industrial production is ASML's EUV process, that TSMC uses to make, at a maximum, a few nanomater small nodes. This is still an inaccuracy of dozens of atoms, since atoms are often 0.1 nanometers to 0.3. Modern physics research, despite its stagnating status, has produced accuracies several orders of magnitude better.
