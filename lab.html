PROMETHEUS LAB

Experimental Research

Our cutting-edge nanotechnology and experimental physics research division. The divisions of our lab include:

Macroscale Division

Our macrofabrication division produces bulk structures that are often used to produce vital portions for our mesocale and nanoscale apparatus.

Mesoscale Division

Our mesoscale division identifies and produces structures with dimensions between 1 mm and 1 nm. This lab has a large variety of experimental physics and mesofabrication abilities, and includes microfluidics, micro-optics, integrated circuitry, optical microscopy and more.

Nanoscale Division

Our nanoscale division has the resources necessary to accurately produce 0 to 3d nanoscale structures via various deposition and individual methods, as well as do a variety of characterization methods including and various forms of nanoscale-sensitive microscopy and spectroscopy.

Computational Research

We use computational methods to model and simulate nano, pico, femto- and subfemto-scale phenomena, with a focus on versatility and power. Our systems can run programs on all 3 major operating systems, including GPU-accelerated molecular simulation libraries. Open variations of Molecular Renderer, Nanoengineer-1 and libraries such as CERN's ROOT, OpenMM, GROMACS' and more are used in our research.
